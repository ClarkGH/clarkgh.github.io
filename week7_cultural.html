My Values and Stereotype Threat

     Revisiting my values on my first cultural blog entry and connecting it with the previous stereotype threat entry, I found a few ways for me to work past stereotype threat. Mainly finding challenges make me want to work harder, so anytime people assume things about me, I take it as a challenge to take what they're saying, assess if it's good or bad, and then improve upon the good and correct the bad if it ends up being true.

     As long as I have a supportive community of friends around me, and I'm accomplishing great things through hard work, I know I will be just fine with everything at DevBootcamp. I've noticed that working with several people who are already in the same cohort as I am, I was able to grow past where I was initially. Several people have already helped in improving my understanding as well as lifting me where I was with my knowledge in coding.

     One other thing that really helps is feeling like I have accomplished something. The way I get to feel that way is by setting goals and then reaching them. I generally keep a planner with goals written down with objectives that I want to reach by that day. It does get in the way of my personal life if I set too strict of goals, so I make sure to have my goals to be a little loose.

     In terms of exercises that will help me overcome the downtimes that have been, or will be, I have a few things that help me feel great. The first is exercise, I train for and run races, and the adrenaline always makes me feel like I'm accomplishing something. I have a global gym membership at 24 Hour Fitness and I know there's a location close to the DBC program. In addition to exercise, when I feel overwhelmed, I always like to take a moment to myself and meditate. Meditation frees me from a lot of internal pain and helps me to grow.
